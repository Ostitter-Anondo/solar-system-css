A Scaled Model of the Solar System Made Using CSS Animations

Calculation Methodology for The Orbit

Here I used a scaled vesion of orbit times. Following is the method used for calculating the orbit times.

Assuming the orbit time for earth as 1, and scaling the orbit period in a 1 year:10 seconds ratio, the following time can be calculated.

Planet Scale of 1 Scale of 10s
Mercury 0.241 2.41 seconds
Venus 0.615 6.15 seconds
Earth 1 10 seconds
Mars 1.881 18.81 seconds
Jupiter 11.86 118.6 seconds
Saturn 29.46 294.6 seconds
Uranus 84.01 840.1 seconds
Neptune 164.8 1648 seconds

However, as can be seen from the calculations, as we go outwards, the outer planet orbits become so large that they become painfully slow. To combat this, I decided to follow some advice I got from u/ballgag on r/space, where they told me to perhaps try a logarithmic function instead.

So I decided to use 10log(*period on a scale of 10*) seconds, which gave me the following results:

Planet Function Result
Mercury 10log(2.41) 3.82 seconds
Venus 10log(615) 7.89 seconds
Earth 10log(10) 10 seconds
Mars 10log(18.81) 12.74 seconds
Jupiter 10log(118.6) 20.74 seconds
Saturn 10log(294.6) 24.69 seconds
Uranus 10log(840.1) 29.24 seconds
Neptune 10log(1648) 32.7 seconds

Credits to u/ballgag for suggesting the use of logarithmic function.
